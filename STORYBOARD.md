# STORYBOARD

## Sequence Overview

- Target runtime: about 53 seconds.
- Structure: 4 scenes, 9 shots.
- Escalation rule: stillness and observation in Scene 1, invasive forward drift in Scene 2, uninterrupted propulsion in Scene 3, weaponized inevitability in Scene 4.

## Scene 1: House and Woman

### Scene ID and Title

S1 - House and Woman

### Purpose

Establish the house as a real, specific home and the Woman as its quiet center.

### Emotional Beat

Calm, ritual, rootedness.

### Location and Time

Exterior field and interior room of the same rural house, overcast day.

### Who Is Present

The Woman only; the Soldier appears only as a portrait.

### What Changes in the Scene

The house shifts from distant object to intimate interior memory-space. The portrait is introduced as the next threshold.

### Transition In

Open on silence and distance.

### Transition Out

The portrait fills the frame and stops behaving like a wall object.

### Shot List

#### Shot ID

S1-SH01

- Duration: 6 seconds
- Framing: wide exterior establishing shot
- Camera motion: very slow forward drift across the field toward the house
- Subject action: the house sits still in an empty field; no visible human movement outside
- Mood: hushed, solitary, observational
- Continuity dependencies: whitewashed walls, dark pitched roof, single chimney, two front-facing windows, overcast daylight
- Intended cut or transition relationship: opening anchor image; the geometry of the facade must be memorable for the final FPV return
- First frame state: the house is small in frame, surrounded by muted field grass and soft gray sky
- Last frame state: the house is larger and centered, with the front facade clearly legible

#### Shot ID

S1-SH02

- Duration: 8 seconds
- Framing: medium-close interior profile or three-quarter view of the Woman seated and knitting
- Camera motion: subtle locked-off frame or almost imperceptible inward creep
- Subject action: the Woman knits slowly; only her hands, shoulders, and breath move
- Mood: intimate, restrained, lived-in
- Continuity dependencies: same overcast daylight motivated through window light, same wardrobe throughout, portrait visible behind or near her, wool yarn and wooden interior details present
- Intended cut or transition relationship: cut from exterior to interior by matching stillness; this shot establishes the domestic rhythm that the portrait shot will violate
- First frame state: her weathered hands and knitting occupy the visual foreground, room details soft behind
- Last frame state: the portrait becomes more compositionally dominant behind her as the viewer's attention shifts upward

#### Shot ID

S1-SH03

- Duration: 7 seconds
- Framing: portrait-centered push-in, beginning as a medium shot of wall area and ending near full-frame portrait
- Camera motion: deliberate slow push toward the framed portrait
- Subject action: the Woman remains seated and nearly still at the edge of frame or just out of frame; the portrait does not yet move
- Mood: reverent, uneasy, invasive
- Continuity dependencies: portrait frame must look aged and modest; the Soldier's face must match later battlefield identity; room texture and window light remain natural and subdued
- Intended cut or transition relationship: the push becomes the bridge into Scene 2; the final frame should be composed to let the portrait image become the battlefield without a jarring reset
- First frame state: portrait hangs in its room context, clearly a physical object on a plaster wall
- Last frame state: the portrait image nearly fills the screen, with frame edges beginning to disappear into darkness and grain

## Scene 2: Portrait to Battlefield

### Scene ID and Title

S2 - Portrait to Battlefield

### Purpose

Turn memory into lived war and make the Soldier emotionally immediate.

### Emotional Beat

Unease becoming panic.

### Location and Time

The image-world of the portrait opening into an older battlefield, daytime through smoke.

### Who Is Present

The Soldier.

### What Changes in the Scene

A static monochrome image becomes moving monochrome memory, then crosses into color and danger.

### Transition In

Continue the push from the portrait until the image gains depth and breath.

### Transition Out

The Soldier's forward path resolves into the tank surface and then an opening into machinery.

### Shot List

#### Shot ID

S2-SH04

- Duration: 6 seconds
- Framing: tight portrait-to-tableau transformation, starting close on the Soldier's face and widening slightly as depth appears behind him
- Camera motion: continuous forward creep into the portrait plane
- Subject action: the portrait subtly comes alive; smoke or drifting particulate begins to move while the Soldier holds the same pose for a moment before animation starts
- Mood: haunted, suspended, uncanny
- Continuity dependencies: exact facial anchors from the portrait, image begins in black and white, no abrupt change of costume or age
- Intended cut or transition relationship: should feel like the same shot as S1-SH03 completed; last moment cues the Soldier's first real motion into S2-SH05
- First frame state: almost flat monochrome portrait texture, as if seen on paper or canvas
- Last frame state: the Soldier is now inside a dimensional monochrome battlefield with smoke moving around him

#### Shot ID

S2-SH05

- Duration: 10 seconds
- Framing: medium-wide to medium tracking view on the Soldier running forward through battlefield chaos
- Camera motion: urgent forward-tracking pursuit that stays on the Soldier's path
- Subject action: the Soldier runs through smoke, mud, and blast pressure; during the shot the monochrome image gradually blooms into muted color
- Mood: fear, urgency, moral ugliness
- Continuity dependencies: older-war uniform only, same face as portrait, battlefield remains daytime through smoke, forward motion must line up with the tank entrance in the next shot
- Intended cut or transition relationship: the tank must appear on the Soldier's forward line so the cut or seamless passage into Scene 3 feels spatially inevitable rather than symbolic only
- First frame state: the Soldier breaks into motion in near-monochrome, smoke swallowing the background
- Last frame state: muted color is fully present and the tank body dominates the forward path as camera closes on it

## Scene 3: Tank to FPV

### Scene ID and Title

S3 - Tank to FPV

### Purpose

Bridge older mechanized war into contemporary remote violence through uninterrupted forward force.

### Emotional Beat

Compression, disorientation, inevitability.

### Location and Time

Exterior battlefield surface into tank interior space, then out through the barrel into open air above the same rural landscape.

### Who Is Present

No stable human subject; the camera inherits the war machine's trajectory.

### What Changes in the Scene

The viewer stops following a person and becomes the movement of a weapon system.

### Transition In

Enter the tank on the Soldier's forward vector.

### Transition Out

The barrel exit becomes an FPV flight line aimed at the house.

### Shot List

#### Shot ID

S3-SH06

- Duration: 5 seconds
- Framing: extreme forward rush into tank armor and interior tunnel geometry
- Camera motion: aggressive continuous plunge through metal, seams, darkness, and mechanical texture
- Subject action: no human action foregrounded; the shot is about spatial conversion from battlefield exterior into machine interior
- Mood: claustrophobic, mechanistic, unstoppable
- Continuity dependencies: the entry vector must match the end of S2-SH05; tank design should read as older-war hardware, not futuristic; motion stays smooth rather than montage-like
- Intended cut or transition relationship: either hidden cut on darkness/metal or seamless morph; this is the sequence hinge that converts observer perspective into projectile perspective
- First frame state: tank hull fills the frame at close range, color and smoke still visible
- Last frame state: the image resolves into a centered barrel-like tunnel with a bright circular exit ahead

#### Shot ID

S3-SH07

- Duration: 5 seconds
- Framing: first-person barrel exit into low-altitude FPV flight over the field
- Camera motion: explosive release from tunnel into open-air acceleration
- Subject action: the camera bursts from the barrel and stabilizes into contemporary FPV-like forward flight with no interface graphics
- Mood: cold, inhuman, committed
- Continuity dependencies: daylight must match the opening exterior, field orientation should allow the same house to appear in the next shot, no HUD or digital overlays
- Intended cut or transition relationship: this is the modern-war reveal; the last frame must already orient the viewer toward the house corridor used in Scene 4
- First frame state: bright circular exit at the end of the barrel fills the screen
- Last frame state: the field horizon locks in and the house direction becomes spatially readable ahead

## Scene 4: Strike

### Scene ID and Title

S4 - Strike

### Purpose

Return to the opening house and destroy it only after recognition is secure.

### Emotional Beat

Inevitability, dread, shock.

### Location and Time

The same rural field and house from Scene 1, same overcast day.

### Who Is Present

The house as target; the Woman is implied inside, not shown.

### What Changes in the Scene

The remembered home becomes the endpoint of a weapon trajectory.

### Transition In

Maintain uninterrupted FPV motion from the barrel exit.

### Transition Out

End on obliteration and debris bloom, not aftermath coverage.

### Shot List

#### Shot ID

S4-SH08

- Duration: 7 seconds
- Framing: low fast FPV approach on the house, slightly rising as needed to keep facade legible
- Camera motion: relentless forward rush with slight tactical corrections, no ornamental flourishes
- Subject action: the house rapidly grows in frame; grass, fence, or ground texture streaks by underneath
- Mood: predatory, helpless, terminal
- Continuity dependencies: house architecture must exactly match S1-SH01, daylight and field palette remain consistent, the shot must hold recognition for at least a beat before impact
- Intended cut or transition relationship: the purpose is confirmation; the end of the shot should place the viewer milliseconds before collision so the final shot can either continue seamlessly or cut on imminent contact
- First frame state: the house is visible ahead at distance, clearly the same building from the opening
- Last frame state: the facade nearly fills frame, windows and wall texture identifiable just before impact

#### Shot ID

S4-SH09

- Duration: 4 seconds
- Framing: impact close with immediate engulfing detonation
- Camera motion: uninterrupted collision path ending in violent bloom and image breakup
- Subject action: collision triggers an orange-white explosion, debris, dust, and collapsing fragments; readability lasts a fraction before obliteration
- Mood: brutal, final, anti-spectacle shock
- Continuity dependencies: impact must occur on the same facade established in S4-SH08; explosion should not stylize into blockbuster fireball; the house must still be recognizable at the first instant of contact
- Intended cut or transition relationship: terminal end shot; no need for a separate aftermath unless later revision demands it
- First frame state: the front wall and window area are still recognizable at the point of imminent contact
- Last frame state: the frame is consumed by blast light, dust, and debris with orientation collapsing into white-orange chaos
