# STORYBOARD

## SCENE 1: THE HOUSE HOLDS MEMORY

### Scene ID and Title

S1 - The House Holds Memory

### Purpose

Establish the house as a place of peace, age, and continuity before it becomes the target.

### Emotional Beat

Stillness with buried grief.

### Location and Time

Rural field and peasant-house interior, overcast day, late afternoon.

### Who Is Present

The Woman.

### What Changes in the Scene

The house moves from being simple setting to being emotionally charged space. The portrait becomes the gateway to the rest of the film.

### Transition In

Cold open.

### Transition Out

Slow push into the portrait until the portrait fills the frame and becomes live action.

### Shot List

#### SHOT 1

- Shot ID: S1-SH1
- Framing: Wide exterior establishing shot.
- Camera Motion: Very slow forward drift or almost imperceptible push.
- Subject Action: The house sits alone in a broad field; grass moves lightly in the wind.
- Mood: Quiet, vulnerable, timeless.
- Continuity Dependencies: The roofline, chimney, window placement, and field approach path must be clear enough to recognize later from the FPV angle.
- Intended Cut or Transition Relationship: Clean cut to interior after the house image has imprinted in the viewer's memory.

#### SHOT 2

- Shot ID: S1-SH2
- Framing: Medium-wide interior shot.
- Camera Motion: Static or subtle inward creep.
- Subject Action: The Woman sits knitting. The portrait of the Soldier hangs visible behind her.
- Mood: Intimate, patient, worn by time.
- Continuity Dependencies: Her wardrobe, knitting, room geography, and portrait placement must remain fixed.
- Intended Cut or Transition Relationship: The portrait must already feel visually magnetic before the next move.

#### SHOT 3

- Shot ID: S1-SH3
- Framing: Push from medium detail into portrait until it dominates frame.
- Camera Motion: Slow precise dolly or simulated lens push.
- Subject Action: Knitting continues softly in the edge of frame before disappearing; the portrait takes over.
- Mood: Unease entering memory.
- Continuity Dependencies: The portrait face must match the later battlefield soldier.
- Intended Cut or Transition Relationship: Match transition from still portrait grain to battlefield monochrome texture.

## SCENE 2: MEMORY BECOMES WAR

### Scene ID and Title

S2 - Memory Becomes War

### Purpose

Reveal the husband as living wartime presence and shift the film from remembrance to danger.

### Emotional Beat

Shock and escalation.

### Location and Time

Battlefield from an older war era, daylight obscured by smoke.

### Who Is Present

The Soldier.

### What Changes in the Scene

The portrait becomes motion, monochrome becomes color, and historical distance collapses.

### Transition In

The final texture of the portrait becomes battlefield air and smoke.

### Transition Out

The camera chooses the tank as the tunnel into the next form of warfare.

### Shot List

#### SHOT 4

- Shot ID: S2-SH4
- Framing: Medium shot resolving into a wider battlefield action view.
- Camera Motion: Forward tracking with the Soldier as he runs.
- Subject Action: The Soldier runs from an explosion; a tank is visible in the background.
- Mood: Panic, survival, disorientation.
- Continuity Dependencies: Starts in black and white, then blooms into desaturated color at the explosion flash; the Soldier must read as the portrait man.
- Intended Cut or Transition Relationship: The tank should become the strongest visual mass in frame so the next shot can commit to it.

#### SHOT 5

- Shot ID: S2-SH5
- Framing: Aggressive forward move into the side of the tank.
- Camera Motion: Fast, unnatural flight straight toward armor plating.
- Subject Action: The battlefield blurs as the camera dives into the tank body.
- Mood: Claustrophobic inevitability.
- Continuity Dependencies: Motion vector must stay forward and coherent with the previous shot's momentum.
- Intended Cut or Transition Relationship: Hidden stitch into an abstract interior steel passage.

## SCENE 3: OLD MACHINE TO NEW WEAPON

### Scene ID and Title

S3 - Old Machine to New Weapon

### Purpose

Bridge tank warfare to modern drone warfare without verbal explanation.

### Emotional Beat

Acceleration and dread.

### Location and Time

Interior mechanical tunnel transforming into present-day aerial space.

### Who Is Present

No visible human subject; the camera itself becomes the active force.

### What Changes in the Scene

The viewer's position shifts from witness to projectile.

### Transition In

Continuation of the tank dive.

### Transition Out

Barrel exit becomes open-air FPV trajectory toward the house.

### Shot List

#### SHOT 6

- Shot ID: S3-SH6
- Framing: Point-of-view tunnel shot through the tank interior and out the barrel.
- Camera Motion: Fast linear flight.
- Subject Action: Dark steel tunnel, brief flashes of light, then sudden emergence into daylight.
- Mood: Mechanical, predatory.
- Continuity Dependencies: The final exit direction must align with the field geography seen in the opening.
- Intended Cut or Transition Relationship: Seamless emergence into FPV as if the barrel birthed the drone.

#### SHOT 7

- Shot ID: S3-SH7
- Framing: FPV aerial rush, low over the field.
- Camera Motion: High-speed forward attack trajectory.
- Subject Action: The house appears ahead and rapidly grows in frame.
- Mood: Cold, unstoppable, horrifying.
- Continuity Dependencies: The house silhouette, field layout, and daylight must unmistakably match Shot 1.
- Intended Cut or Transition Relationship: Direct continuation into impact or a one-frame hard cut at the instant before impact.

## SCENE 4: HOME AS TARGET

### Scene ID and Title

S4 - Home as Target

### Purpose

Deliver the thesis image: the home first seen as sanctuary is destroyed by the war the film traced forward.

### Emotional Beat

Shock and tragic finality.

### Location and Time

Same rural house and field, same day.

### Who Is Present

The house; the Woman is implied inside whether or not she is shown.

### What Changes in the Scene

Sanctuary becomes casualty.

### Transition In

Immediate continuation from FPV approach.

### Transition Out

End on blast, smoke, or brief post-impact hold.

### Shot List

#### SHOT 8

- Shot ID: S4-SH8
- Framing: Impact view or near-impact view of the house.
- Camera Motion: Final committed rush into detonation.
- Subject Action: The house explodes.
- Mood: Violent, devastating, not triumphant.
- Continuity Dependencies: Explosion scale must preserve house recognition for at least a beat before destruction.
- Intended Cut or Transition Relationship: End title or hard cut to black.
