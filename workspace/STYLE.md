# STYLE

## Tone and Mood

Lyrical, mournful, restrained, then suddenly violent. The film should feel like memory hardening into impact.

## Genre

Poetic anti-war short with surreal transition logic grounded in tactile realism.

## Cinematic References

- Tarkovsky-like patience in the house and field imagery.
- Come and See-level panic and moral ugliness once war becomes active.
- Contemporary FPV war footage energy, but stripped of interface graphics and documentary clutter.

## Palette

- Opening: straw, weathered white, old wood brown, faded red, soot gray.
- Battlefield: ash black, mud brown, rust, smoke gray, fire orange.
- FPV section: muted natural field color with cold steel undertone, then sudden orange-white detonation.

## Lighting Style

- Exterior house: soft overcast daylight.
- Interior: natural window light with gentle falloff, no glamorous bounce.
- Battlefield: smoke-broken light, flashes, intermittent fire glow.
- FPV approach: daylight consistent with the opening field so the house is instantly recognizable.

## Texture and Material Cues

Wool yarn, aged plaster, raw wood, lace curtain, tarnished picture frame, mud, soot, steel, smoke, scorched dust.

## Lens and Camera Language

- Opening: deliberate, stable, observational frames.
- Portrait move: slow invasive push.
- Battlefield: handheld or stabilized chaos with forward propulsion.
- Tank transition: impossible but smooth spatial tunnel.
- FPV: low, fast, committed, weapon-like forward motion.

## Movement Style

The camera begins like a patient witness and ends like an unstoppable projectile.

## Editing Rhythm

Long breath at the start, compression in the middle, acceleration at the end. Each shot should inherit momentum from the one before it.

## Forbidden Stylistic Drift

- No glossy blockbuster spectacle.
- No clean CGI shine.
- No romantic sepia nostalgia.
- No stylized game HUD, targeting overlay, or digital graphics.
- No tonal irony.
