# STORYBOARD

## Sequence Summary

A short, deadpan therapy-room exchange unfolds with clean comedic timing: the panda opens with a vulnerable confession, the psychologist absorbs it without rushing, then lands a warm but incisive final line. The visual approach stays minimal and controlled so each framing shift sharpens the joke and the emotional read.

## Shots

### SHOT-01 - Panda opens up

Purpose: Establish the therapy-room setup, the panda’s emotional state, and the core conflict in one readable comedic image.

Visual: Medium wide in a minimal therapy room. A panda lies on a therapy couch, slightly slouched, facing the psychologist. The psychologist sits beside her in a chair near frame edge, partially cropped so the panda remains the visual center. Clean neutral room, restrained composition, gentle daylight or soft practical lighting. The panda says: "Doctor, everyone hates me. Either because of my black side or my white side."

Transition: Opening shot. Hold long enough for the line to land without rushing.

### SHOT-02 - Psychologist absorbs it

Purpose: Create comedic pause and show the psychologist’s calm, measured rhythm before the question.

Visual: Medium shot on the psychologist. She takes a small sip from her mug and sets it down with deliberate calm. No dialogue. The room remains minimal, with the panda implied off-camera.

Transition: Straight cut from the opening confession into a reaction beat. Let the action breathe slightly.

### SHOT-03 - The question

Purpose: Narrow the conflict to self-perception and set up the punchline.

Visual: Close-up on the psychologist. She looks at the panda with composed curiosity and asks: "And which one do you like better?" The frame is still and uncluttered, with eye line and timing carrying the scene.

Transition: Cut in tighter to increase focus and sharpen the setup.

### SHOT-04 - Panda answer

Purpose: Deliver the emotional truth beneath the joke with a more animated response from the panda.

Visual: Medium close-up on the panda. She answers with stronger, desperate gestures than before—hands lifting, body slightly pitching forward, expression earnest and frustrated rather than broad or theatrical. She says: "Neither."

Transition: Cut back to the panda for contrast, letting her physicality briefly break the psychologist’s stillness.

### SHOT-05 - Where we start

Purpose: Land the final line with warmth, authority, and a subtle emotional release.

Visual: Medium close-up tightening to close-up with a slow dolly-in toward the psychologist. She glances up from her notes, adjusts her glasses, and delivers the line with calm precision: "Then that’s where we start." The slow push-in gives the final beat quiet weight rather than melodrama.

Transition: Clean cut into the final shot, then a slow inward move to end on the line.
