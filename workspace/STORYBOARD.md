# STORYBOARD

## Sequence Summary

A frantic Renaissance merchant rides a donkey across Florence with the visual grammar of a political thriller, gripping a satchel as if the fate of the city depends on it. Each beat escalates the sense of urgency as he pushes through market chaos and past the rising Duomo, until he finally dismounts and charges through a doorway at peak intensity. The punchline snaps all that grandeur into embarrassment: he is only returning an overdue library book, and the donkey, visible through the window, is more interested in eating flowers than honoring the drama.

## Shots

### SHOT-01 - Urgent departure

Purpose: Establish the merchant’s panic immediately and frame the errand as absurdly high-stakes.

Visual: Medium-wide moving shot on a narrow Florentine street as the merchant rides his small donkey toward camera through morning bustle, hunched forward and clutching the leather satchel to his chest. Pedestrians and carts give him just enough space to pass. His face is strained, focused, and slightly sweaty; the donkey remains practical and calm.

Transition: Opening shot. Enter with immediate momentum and dramatic seriousness.

### SHOT-02 - Through the market crush

Purpose: Escalate urgency and show the city resisting his progress.

Visual: Side-tracking shot as the donkey threads between crowded market stalls, hanging fabrics, baskets, and startled vendors. The merchant twists around obstacles with mounting alarm, protecting the satchel with both arms whenever someone gets too close. The donkey moves steadily rather than heroically fast, which makes the merchant’s panic feel even bigger.

Transition: Hard continuation from the opening; keep the motion and direction consistent so the rush feels uninterrupted.

### SHOT-03 - Florence opens up

Purpose: Widen the scale and give the chase a grand historical backdrop.

Visual: Wider shot as merchant and donkey cross a more open stretch of Florence with the Duomo under construction looming behind them, cranes, scaffolds, and unfinished masonry visible. The score feels like it should belong to a war courier. The merchant scans ahead with near-desperate intensity while the satchel stays pinned tightly to his side.

Transition: Continue the movement but open the frame, making the errand feel momentarily epic and civic in scale.

### SHOT-04 - Final approach

Purpose: Push the sequence to its highest dramatic pitch just before the reveal.

Visual: Forward-rushing shot as the merchant spots his destination ahead. He yanks the donkey to a stop outside a sober public building, swings down in a scramble, nearly stumbling, then rushes for the entrance without relaxing his grip on the satchel. The donkey lands in place, unfazed.

Transition: Crescendo into a near-climactic arrival; this should feel like the last seconds before a state emergency is averted.

### SHOT-05 - The truth inside

Purpose: Deliver the comic reveal and collapse the false stakes instantly.

Visual: Interior library shot. The merchant stands breathless at a desk, presenting a single overdue book from the satchel with apologetic urgency. Across from him, the librarian receives it with controlled, unimpressed stillness and a withering look. The room is orderly, quiet, and anti-climactic compared with the chase.

Transition: Hard cut from the doorway burst into stillness. The comedic force comes from the abrupt drop in scale.

### SHOT-06 - Donkey button

Purpose: Land the final joke and give the scene one last visual indignity.

Visual: Hold in or near the library interior so the donkey is visible through the window outside, casually eating someone’s flowers while the merchant remains trapped under the librarian’s silent judgment. The contrast between social embarrassment inside and animal indifference outside gives the ending its last sting.

Transition: Gentle comic hold after the reveal, functioning as the final button rather than a new burst of action.
