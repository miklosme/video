# CHARACTERS

## THE WOMAN

### Name and Role

The Woman. Elderly widow or implied widow. Keeper of the home and living witness of war's afterlife.

### Age Range or Vibe

Late 70s to mid 80s. Quiet, grounded, weathered, dignified.

### Physical Appearance Anchors

- Deeply lined face.
- Strong cheekbones.
- Worn working hands emphasized in close detail.
- Gray hair mostly covered by a headscarf.
- Rural, sun-worn skin rather than polished beauty styling.

### Wardrobe Anchors

- Traditional folk costume or folk-inspired rural dress.
- Headscarf.
- Apron.
- Layered blouse or vest with subtle embroidery.
- Muted natural fabrics: off-white, faded red, dark green, brown, charcoal.

### Movement and Behavior Anchors

- Minimal movement.
- Slow knitting rhythm.
- Seated posture with the weight of age.
- No theatrical grief; emotion is contained.

### Emotional Baseline

Habitual calm with an undertow of long-held loss.

### Non-Negotiable Consistency Rules

- She must always feel rooted to the house, not styled like costume drama glamour.
- Her hands and posture should communicate age convincingly.
- She does not become physically present on the battlefield.

### How She Should Be Described in Prompts

Use concrete descriptors: elderly rural woman, weathered hands, traditional folk clothing, quiet domestic stillness, seated and knitting.

### How She Should Not Be Described in Prompts

Avoid fantasy peasant wording, fairy-tale warmth, fashion language, or melodramatic mourning.

## THE SOLDIER

### Name and Role

The Soldier. The younger husband in portrait and battlefield form. He is the connective tissue between memory and war.

### Age Range or Vibe

Mid 20s to early 30s. Lean, farm-bred, human rather than heroic.

### Physical Appearance Anchors

- Same eye shape and facial structure implied by the portrait.
- Lean build.
- Serious face, not movie-star handsome.
- Dirt, sweat, smoke, and strain once on the battlefield.

### Wardrobe Anchors

- Historically grounded military uniform from an older war era.
- Practical field wear, worn and dirty.
- No decorative medals or parade styling.

### Movement and Behavior Anchors

- Running under pressure, survival-driven.
- Looks like a man trying to stay alive, not posing for heroism.

### Emotional Baseline

Fear, urgency, and determination to keep moving.

### Non-Negotiable Consistency Rules

- The portrait version and battlefield version must clearly be the same person.
- He must not read as a generic modern special-operations soldier.
- His battlefield image begins in monochrome before shifting to color.

### How He Should Be Described in Prompts

Young soldier husband, older-war uniform, smoke, mud, strain, running from an explosion.

### How He Should Not Be Described in Prompts

Avoid triumphant hero framing, action-movie coolness, or glossy military fetishism.
