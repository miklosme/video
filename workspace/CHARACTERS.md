# CHARACTERS

## The Merchant

Character ID: merchant

Role: The anxious protagonist whose self-imposed sense of epic urgency drives the entire joke.

Visual anchors:

- Lean middle-aged Florentine man with a narrow, expressive face, tired eyes, prominent nose, short dark wavy hair, and a trimmed beard or stubble.
- Wears a practical but slightly respectable Renaissance merchant outfit: dark wool doublet, muted hose, weathered leather boots, and a worn cap or soft hat suitable for travel.
- Always carries a worn leather satchel clutched close to his body as if it contains something dangerously important.
- Overall silhouette should read as slightly hunched forward, taut, and compressed by stress rather than heroic.

Behavior anchors:

- Moves with breathless, urgent determination, always leaning forward as though late by several hours.
- Constantly scans ahead, grips the satchel protectively, and reacts to minor obstacles as if they are catastrophic setbacks.
- Treats his errand with deadly seriousness, which makes the later reveal funnier.
- Should feel sympathetic rather than foolish: earnest, overcommitted, and quietly ridiculous.

Tone-appropriate personality notes:

- He has the emotional intensity of a man delivering state secrets, but the actual stakes are embarrassingly mundane.
- His comedy comes from conviction, not winkiness; he does not know he is in a joke.
- He should read as frazzled, conscientious, and deeply afraid of social judgment.

Prompt wording:

- Use: clean single-subject Renaissance merchant reference, readable face, full or near-full body, stable dark wool travel clothing, worn leather satchel, soft even lighting, plain or seamless background, neutral pose or slight three-quarter view.
- Avoid: heroic action framing, crowd scenes, dramatic chase imagery, cinematic city backgrounds, weapons, luxury fabrics, or props beyond the satchel and simple travel clothing.

## The Donkey

Character ID: donkey

Role: The merchant’s steadfast mount and the story’s visual comic counterweight.

Visual anchors:

- Small sturdy donkey with soft brown-gray coat, darker mane, large ears, gentle eyes, and a compact practical build.
- Simple rope or leather tack, believable for Renaissance street travel; no ornate decoration.
- Clear, recognizable silhouette with upright ears and slightly shaggy texture.
- Should feel calm, ordinary, and materially unimpressed by the urgency around it.

Behavior anchors:

- Carries the merchant without sharing his panic.
- Moves with practical steadiness rather than speed-driven heroism.
- In the final button, casually eats flowers with complete indifference to the social disaster unfolding nearby.
- Its stillness or mild stubbornness should contrast with the merchant’s frenzy.

Tone-appropriate personality notes:

- The donkey is not a gag animal performing tricks; the humor comes from its normalcy.
- It acts like this is just another day, which quietly punctures the merchant’s grand drama.
- It should feel dependable, placid, and faintly opportunistic.

Prompt wording:

- Use: clean single-subject donkey reference, full body visible, soft even lighting, plain or seamless background, clear tack, natural standing pose, readable markings and silhouette.
- Avoid: rider included, market or street setting, exaggerated expressions, costumes, ornate harnesses, collage layouts, or comedic props.

## The Librarian

Character ID: librarian

Role: The deadpan authority figure who punctures the merchant’s entire illusion with a single look.

Visual anchors:

- Older Florentine librarian, likely late middle age or elderly, with composed posture, lined face, sharp observant eyes, and a severe but controlled expression.
- Wears modest dark academic or clerical Renaissance robes with a simple head covering or tightly arranged hair, depending on gender presentation chosen in visual development.
- Neat, spare silhouette that feels ordered and immovable.
- Hands should feel precise and restrained, suited to handling books and enforcing rules.

Behavior anchors:

- Barely moves, conserving energy while everyone else overperforms.
- Responds to the merchant’s breathless arrival with calm disapproval rather than surprise.
- Delivers judgment through stillness, eye contact, and a tiny shift in expression.
- Feels like someone who has seen every excuse before and is not impressed by theatrics.

Tone-appropriate personality notes:

- Dry, exacting, and socially devastating without ever raising their voice.
- The character is funny because they refuse the scale of the merchant’s performance.
- Should feel credible, not cartoonishly cruel: a guardian of order mildly offended by chaos.

Prompt wording:

- Use: clean single-subject Renaissance librarian reference, readable face and hands, modest dark scholarly clothing, soft even lighting, plain or seamless background, neutral standing or seated pose, calm direct expression.
- Avoid: stacks of books as cluttered scene dressing, dramatic gothic lighting, exaggerated scowls, fantasy wizard styling, ornate accessories, or extra characters.
