# CHARACTERS

## Hero Dog

Character ID: hero-dog

Role: The single on-screen animal protagonist of the advert, used to carry the setup, the eating moment, and the transformation payoff.

Visual anchors:

- Medium-sized dog with a clean, friendly face, floppy ears, expressive eyes, and an easy-to-read silhouette.
- Same dog before and after transformation, with coat color as the only major change: pure white at first, deep solid black after eating.
- Healthy, well-groomed coat, proportionate build, no clothing, no collar unless later required for branding.

Behavior anchors:

- Curious, eager, food-motivated, and immediately drawn to the bowl.
- Gentle, alert, and appealing rather than chaotic; movements should read as happy anticipation and then satisfied calm.
- After the transformation, the dog appears confident, healthy, and content.

Prompt wording:

- Use: clean single-dog reference phrasing, readable face, full body or three-quarter body visibility, stable proportions, soft even lighting, plain or seamless background, consistent ear shape and expression.
- Avoid: multiple dogs, split-screen before/after compositions, costumes, dramatic action poses, busy home environments, extreme closeups, or any wording that changes breed, size, or facial structure.

## Dog Food Pack

Character ID: dog-food-pack

Role: The product presence in the advert, shown clearly near the end and optionally hinted earlier through bowl setup.

Visual anchors:

- Clean, modern dog food packaging with a premium but simple advert-ready look.
- Readable pack silhouette with a tidy label area that can remain generic or branded later.
- Packaging should feel contemporary, trustworthy, and easy to read in a short-form ad.

Behavior anchors:

- Static product object used as a clear end-frame branding anchor.
- Always presented neatly and intentionally, never crumpled or incidental.

Prompt wording:

- Use: single product pack on clean background or placed cleanly beside bowl/dog in ad composition, front-facing or slight three-quarter angle, soft commercial lighting, minimal clutter.
- Avoid: fake dense label text, damaged packaging, multiple product variants, crowded shelf scenes, or distracting extra props.
