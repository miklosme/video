# STORY

## Logline

A panda brings her identity crisis to a psychologist and discovers the real starting point is not choosing sides, but accepting the whole of herself.

## Synopsis

In a simple, intimate therapy-room setup, a panda admits that she feels hated for both her black side and her white side. The psychologist asks which side she likes better, expecting a preference or confession. The panda answers, "Neither," and the psychologist gently reframes the conversation: that is exactly where they begin. The joke lands as a quiet emotional turn—less about solving the panda and more about giving her permission to stop splitting herself apart.

## Beginning

We open on a restrained, clinical therapy setting: a panda across from a calm psychologist. The panda shares her complaint plainly and vulnerably, making the conflict immediately clear—she feels rejected from both directions and has internalized the problem as a personal failure.

## Middle

The psychologist responds with a simple, disarming question: which side does she like better? The question narrows the issue to self-perception, forcing the panda to confront the fact that she has been trying to rank or separate parts of herself rather than understand them.

## End

The panda answers, "Neither," and the psychologist delivers the punchline with warmth: "Then that’s where we start." The final beat leaves us with a small emotional release—the panda is not asked to pick a side, only to begin from self-acceptance.
