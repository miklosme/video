# STORY

An elderly woman sits alone in a rural house, quietly knitting while the room holds the stillness of a long memory. Behind her hangs a portrait of her soldier husband, a visual anchor that connects the present domestic interior to the past.

The camera moves toward the portrait and enters it, crossing from the woman’s color-saturated present into a wartime memory that begins in black and white and gradually shifts into color chaos. The soldier’s world is tense and unstable, moving through the battlefield until the camera is drawn into a tank.

From inside the tank, the camera passes through the barrel and emerges into a modern FPV-style flight. The perspective becomes fast, aggressive, and airborne as it races back toward the same rural house from the opening image.

The final movement ends in impact: the house is struck and explodes, collapsing the distance between private memory, war, and present destruction into one continuous shot.

## Narrative arc

- Quiet domestic solitude
- Memory framed by a portrait
- Immersion into wartime past
- Transition from historical war imagery to modern drone perspective
- Return to the house as target
- Explosive ending
