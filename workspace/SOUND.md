# SOUND

## Purpose

This file holds the film's sound direction, sonic continuity, and key audio decisions. Keep it focused on what the viewer should hear, what should stay absent, and how sound carries the emotional arc.

## Sound Arc

The film should begin in near-silence, grounded in room tone and tactile domestic detail, then gradually admit pressure, air, steel, and violence until the ending becomes overwhelming and abrupt.

Calm domestic intimacy -> haunted threshold -> battlefield panic -> mechanical tunnel compression -> inhuman FPV rush -> impact shock.

## Core Sound Language

- Opening field: soft wind, distant ambient countryside, no sentimental music swell.
- Interior room: wool friction, knitting needles, chair creak, room tone, muted window-air presence.
- Portrait threshold: let ordinary room sound thin out as particulate, breath, and distant pressure emerge.
- Battlefield: muffled concussive force, smoke-heavy air, mud movement, panic without macho spectacle.
- Tank interior: metallic resonance, tunnel pressure, mechanical scrape, narrowed frequency range.
- FPV approach: aggressive air rush and directional forward pressure, but no interface beeps or drone UI sound design.
- Impact: one brutal detonation event with debris and overpressure, then immediate cutoff or suffocating dust-heavy decay.

## Music Guidance

- If music exists, keep it sparse and textural rather than melodic or sentimental.
- Avoid heroic war scoring, triumph, or tragic-string overstatement.
- Silence is allowed to do a lot of the work.

## Forbidden Drift

- No blockbuster boom-sweetener stack.
- No documentary news-audio overlays.
- No glitchy sci-fi drone sound design.
- No manipulative nostalgic folk cue that romanticizes the home.
