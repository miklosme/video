# PROMPT-PACK

## Status

First-pass working prompt pack. Keep canon fixed and adapt wording after a specific video model is chosen.

## Project-Wide Prompt Anchors

- Short anti-war visual poem.
- Unnamed rural Central or Eastern European setting.
- Whitewashed peasant house with dark pitched roof and single chimney in an open field.
- Elderly rural woman in muted folk clothing, headscarf, apron, weathered hands, knitting in silence.
- Husband exists as an aged portrait and as the same younger soldier on an older battlefield.
- Start in stillness, end in violent forward momentum.
- Visual tone: lyrical realism turning into nightmare, never glossy action spectacle.

## Character Prompt Anchors

### The Woman

Elderly rural woman, late 70s to 80s, deeply lined face, gray hair under a headscarf, muted folk dress with apron, weathered hands, seated and knitting, quiet contained sadness, natural window light, no glamour styling.

### The Soldier

Young soldier husband in an older-war uniform, same facial structure as the portrait, lean build, mud and smoke on face, running to survive, not heroic, battlefield panic, initially monochrome then desaturated color.

## Transition Anchors

- Portrait grain becomes battlefield air and smoke.
- Explosion flash motivates black-and-white to color shift.
- Tank side becomes entry tunnel.
- Barrel exit becomes FPV launch.
- Final field geometry must echo the opening establishing shot.

## Shot Prompts

### SHOT 1

Wide exterior of a lone whitewashed rural house with a dark pitched roof and one chimney in an open field under soft overcast daylight, sparse wind moving through grass, quiet and vulnerable atmosphere, restrained cinematic realism, hold long enough for the house shape and field layout to register.

### SHOT 2

Interior of a modest peasant-house room, elderly woman in muted traditional folk clothing and headscarf sitting and knitting, weathered hands visible, natural side window light, aged plaster walls and wood furniture, framed portrait of her soldier husband clearly visible behind her, intimate stillness, no sentimentality.

### SHOT 3

Slow push from the woman toward the framed portrait of her young soldier husband, knitting fades out of frame, the portrait becoming dominant and uncanny, gentle domestic realism turning into uneasy memory, preserve the soldier's face clearly enough to match later.

### SHOT 4

The portrait resolves into a living battlefield scene, the same young soldier now running from an explosion with a tank visible behind him, the image begins in black and white and shifts into desaturated color at the blast flash, mud, smoke, fear, survival energy, anti-war tone, no heroic posing.

### SHOT 5

Fast forward rush into the side of the tank as battlefield chaos blurs around the frame, steel armor filling view, the camera commits to the machine as if entering history's mechanism of violence, claustrophobic and inevitable.

### SHOT 6

Point-of-view flight through a dark steel tunnel inside the tank, brief flashes of light on metal surfaces, continuous forward acceleration, then sudden emergence through the barrel into open daylight, seamless transformation from old war machine to modern weapon perspective.

### SHOT 7

Low fast FPV rush over the same rural field seen at the start, daylight matching the opening shot, the same whitewashed house ahead growing quickly larger, cold mechanical attack trajectory, no HUD graphics, no text overlays, pure forward dread.

### SHOT 8

Final committed impact on the same house, let the house remain recognizable for a beat before detonation, then violent explosion and debris, tragic not triumphant, end on blast, smoke, or immediate black.

## Negative Guidance

- No blockbuster heroism.
- No glossy CGI polish.
- No romantic nostalgia haze.
- No modern interior objects.
- No military interface overlays or gaming aesthetics.
- No mismatch between the opening house and the final target house.

## Next Adaptation Step

Once a target video model is chosen, rewrite each shot with that model's preferred motion, camera, and prompt syntax while preserving the canon above.
