# CONTINUITY

## Immutable Canon Checkpoints

- The opening house and the final exploded house are the same building.
- The portrait man and the battlefield soldier are the same person.
- The Woman remains in the house timeline and does not physically enter the war sequence.
- The war progression is intentional: portrait memory -> older battlefield -> tank -> contemporary FPV strike.

## Wardrobe Continuity

- The Woman keeps the same folk clothing, headscarf, and apron throughout all interior appearances.
- The Soldier's uniform must remain historically older-war in both portrait and battlefield imagery.

## Props Continuity

- Knitting needles and yarn are present in the interior and should feel handmade, not modern synthetic.
- The portrait frame should be aged, modest, and recognizable.

## Environment Continuity

- House exterior anchor details: whitewashed walls, dark pitched roof, one chimney, two front-facing windows, isolated field setting.
- Interior anchor details: wooden furniture, plaster walls, portrait visible behind or near the Woman, soft window light, no modern decor.

## Lighting and Time-of-Day Continuity

- Opening exterior and final FPV strike occur under matching overcast daylight.
- Interior lighting remains natural and subdued.
- Battlefield light can fluctuate through smoke and fire, but should not suggest night.

## Emotional Continuity

- The Woman's interior calm should imply long-practiced endurance, not immediate panic.
- The Soldier's energy is survival-focused.
- The FPV section must feel inhumanly committed, almost emotionless.

## Motion Direction and Screen Direction

- The camera's dominant vector becomes increasingly forward from Shot 3 onward.
- The Soldier should run in a way that allows the tank to sit on the forward path into the next shot.
- The barrel exit should orient the camera toward the same field approach used to identify the house in Shot 1.

## Open Continuity Risks

- The portrait and battlefield actor may drift apart in facial identity.
- The house may change shape between pastoral establishing shot and FPV targeting shot.
- The older tank to modern drone transition could read as random unless the forward motion is seamless.
- The explosion may obscure recognition too early if it detonates before the house registers.

## Mitigation Notes

- Reuse the same facial anchors for portrait and battlefield prompts.
- Keep exterior architecture description identical in every house-related prompt.
- Treat the tank interior and barrel as a single directional tunnel, not a montage gag.
- Let the FPV shot hold the recognizable house for a beat before impact.
